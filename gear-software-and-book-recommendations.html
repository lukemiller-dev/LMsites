Gear, Software, and Book Recommendations

Luke Miller | Recommendations | June 1, 2017

Gear & Software

Webflow

Ran Segall introduced me to Webflow. It has the same great features as Wordpress, but the UX is much more designer/developer friendly and  they have added a lot of client billing and team collaboration features along with a huge network of users who help each other answer questions on their blog. Lead designers from companies like Google, Intuit, and Yelp agree. Ran has some great comparison reviews against some other build it yourself sites like Wordpress and Wix and he can explain all of the Webflow advantages much better than I can, so check out his reviews here.

Go to Webflow

Affinity Photo

Please do not spend a ton of money on Adobe Photoshop. I was so stubborn about not paying for Photoshop that I used to crop and edit images in Google Docs. Yeah I know that's pretty pathetic but I didn't want to pay all that money for photoshop when I was really only editing a few things for my images like color and size. Eventually I found Affinity. It has all of the same crazy features for graphic designers and photographers while maintaining the same great UX as Photoshop, unlike free services like Gimp. Affinity is only a ONE TIME payment of $50. Let me say that again.... ONE TIME PAYMENT. It has awards from all the big guys like Apple and their website is awesome too, so even if you don't want the software you should check them out.

Go to Affinity

UXPin

UXPin is one of the best tools you can use for wireframes, web design. I was able to pick up the process very quickly because the UI is very similar to Webflow's, but even if you are just starting, it's easy to figure out and they have you run through a tutorial before to get down the fundamentals. They have also added an interaction feature that lets you add basic user interactions like clicks and hovers. It's also a very good substitute for Sketch if you are using a windows machine. You can do a free trial if you want to tool around with it.

Go to UXPin

LyxPro Headphones

I've had these for about a year and they're great. Don't spend $400 on Beats when you can get the same headphones for a fraction of the price.

See them on Amazon

Acer 21.5 inch Monitor

I'm not too picky when it comes to monitors. This one isn't too pricey and the picture is very clear. This one will get the job done for you.

See it on Amazon

Reading Recommendations

Total Recall

Arnold Schwarzenegger is one of the most extraordinary people on the planet. The story of his life is captivating and he relates so much of his success to reps and consistency. His story is the epitome of the American dream and he leaves you with a no excuse attitude.

Get it on Amazon

#AskGaryVee

Gary Vaynerchuck has become an entrepreneurial icon. This audio book really breaks down online marketing, the entrepreneurial mindset, and the importance of self awareness and execution.  This one of my favorites from an entrepreneurial standpoint.

Get it on Amazon

Elon Musk

Elon Musk is one of the most innovative and gifted minds of our era. Ashlee Vance does an exceptional job of telling the story of his childhood to his falls and triumphs with Tesla and SpaceX. It will be compelling to watch Elon strive towards his most astonishing endeavor of colonizing Mars.

Get it on Amazon

Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs. A fascinating story of how a hippie turned his obsession for the intersection of design and technology into the most profitable company in the world and his mission to put a dent in the universe. A must read for anyone in business or technology.

Get it on Amazon
